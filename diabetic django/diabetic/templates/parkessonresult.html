Here are some common medications used in the management of Parkinson's disease:



Levodopa (L-DOPA):

Levodopa is one of the most effective medications for managing the motor symptoms of Parkinson's disease. It is converted into dopamine in the brain, compensating for the reduced levels of dopamine in individuals with Parkinson's.

MAO-B Inhibitors:

Monoamine oxidase type B (MAO-B) inhibitors help increase dopamine levels by inhibiting the breakdown of dopamine in the brain. Examples include selegiline and rasagiline.

COMT Inhibitors:

Catechol-O-methyltransferase (COMT) inhibitors extend the effects of levodopa by inhibiting its breakdown. An example is entacapone.

Anticholinergics:

These medications can help control tremors in some cases. Examples include trihexyphenidyl and benztropine.

Amantadine:

Amantadine may be used to help control tremors and dyskinesias (involuntary movements) associated with Parkinson's disease.

Duopa:

Duopa is a gel formulation of levodopa and carbidopa that is delivered directly into the small intestine through a pump. It is used for advanced stages of Parkinson's disease.
